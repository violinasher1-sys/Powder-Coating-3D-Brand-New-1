Our Services

Engineered surface protection — from Sa 2.5 blasting to certified powder coating.

01 · Powder Coating®

Electrostatic perfection. Oven-cured toughness.

Our 6-stage powder coating line handles everything from small components to 6.5 × 1.7 × 3 m structures — applying epoxy, polyester, or hybrid powder via electrostatic spray, then curing to a seamless, chemically bonded film.

Process Steps
  • Chemical pre-treatment
  • Rinsing
  • Oven drying
  • Electrostatic powder application
  • Oven curing
  • Final cooling & inspection
Powders Available
  • Epoxy — chemical resistance, indoor use
  • Polyester — UV stability, outdoor exposure
  • Epoxy-Polyester Hybrid — balanced durability & finish
  • National Paints PE & PA 1000 Series (certified)
  • Custom RAL colors on request
Component Capacity
Small components120–180 m²/day
Medium pipes 1.5″~75 m²/day
Large sheets 2.2×2.5247–250 m²/day
Large structures300–350 m²/day
Max component size6.5 × 1.7 × 3 m
QC Standards
  • ASTM D3359 — adhesion (cross-cut)
  • ASTM D7091 — dry-film thickness
  • ASTM B117 — salt-spray resistance
  • Impact resistance test
  • Full batch documentation
02 · Industrial Sandblasting

Surface profile, properly engineered.

Steel-grit blasting for heavy substrates, fine abrasive blasting for detail work — measured to Sa 2.5 cleanliness with a 50–75 μm anchor profile in a 24 m² dedicated blast booth with ~3 tons/day capacity.

Standards Achieved
  • Cleanliness: Sa 2.5 (ISO 8501-1)
  • Anchor profile: 50–75 microns
  • Booth: 24 m² · 6×4×3 m clearance
  • Daily output: ~3 tons of steel
Equipment On-Site
  • Automatic blasting machines
  • Manual blasting chambers
  • Steel-grit recovery & dust collection
  • Diesel air compressor + receiver tank
Available Methods
  • Abrasive sandblasting (steel grit)
  • Solvent cleaning (thinner wash)
  • Component preheating
  • Manual sanding & grinding

Testing to ASTM

04 · Surface Preparation

No coating outlasts the surface beneath it.

Documented prep at every stage — solvent cleaning, sanding, preheating, and pretreatment — verified before the first kilo of powder is sprayed.

01 / SA 2.5

Cleanliness Target

All visible mill scale, rust, and contamination removed. Only slight discolouration permitted per ISO 8501-1.

02 / 50–75 μm

Anchor Profile

Profile depth tuned for adhesion of epoxy/polyester systems and resistance to under-film corrosion.

03 / Pre-treat

Chemical Pretreatment

Phosphate or equivalent conversion in dedicated tanks, followed by rinsing and oven drying.

04 / Inspect

Pre-Coat Inspection

Surface profile gauge, dust tape test, and visual sign-off before the powder line begins.

05 · Industrial Painting

Liquid coatings where powder isn't the answer.

Epoxy, polyurethane, and specialty liquid paints for items requiring flexible coatings, heat resistance, or marine-grade protection.

Coating Systems
  • Epoxy primer + polyurethane topcoat
  • Anti-corrosion multi-layer systems
  • Heat-resistant paints (up to 600°C)
  • Marine-grade finishes
  • Intumescent fire-protection coating
Applications
  • Offshore & marine structures
  • High-temperature piping
  • Internal tank lining
  • Concrete & masonry protection
  • Large items unsuitable for oven
